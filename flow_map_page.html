Geovisualization Portfolio GEOG:3540

This flow map showcases migration patterns within the US from 1887-1924. This map shows that from 1887-1924, cities in the Southern-Central states received the highest volume of new families, whereas the cities on the West Coast, the northern states in the Midwest, and the eastern coast down to Louisiana received noticeably less. Oklahoma received the highest number of Total Flows, the largest portion coming from Texas. The flow pattern connecting Texas and Oklahoma shows that there was a large volume of families moving in between these two states. My map utilizes a tapered flow symbol to increase visual understanding of the flows. The “Total Flows” circle size, as well as the “Migration” flow thickness, are proportional to the data, which allows for easier visual understanding of the patterns.
